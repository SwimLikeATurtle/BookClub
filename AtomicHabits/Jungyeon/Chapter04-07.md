## THE FIRST LAW : MAKE IT OBVIOUS

### Chapter 4 : The Man Who Didn't Look Right
- The human brain is a prediction machine.
	- It is continuously taking in ur surroundings and analyzing the information it comes across.
- You are much more than your conscious self.
- If a habit remains mindless, you can't expect to improve it.
- **Point-and-Calling** : A safety system designed to reduce mistakes by identifying the detail, pointing it and naming it aloud. 
- **The Habit Scorecard**: Make a 'Point-and-Calling' system for our personal lives
	1) Make a list of daily habits
	2) Look at each behavior and ask if it is a good/neutral/bad habit. Label signs next to the behavior accordingly(+/=/-)
	- Does this behavior help me become who I want to be?
	- Good habits = net positive outcomes = healthy long-term behavior
	3) Simply notice the habits.
- If you want to change the bad habits, use 'Point-and-Calling' in your life. This adds weight to the action rather than letting yourself mindlessly slip into old habits
	- e.g. I'm about to eat this cookie but I don't need it. Eating it will cause me to gain weight and hurt my health.


### Chapter 5 : The Best Way to Start a New Habit
- **Implementation Intention**: A plan you make beforehand about when and where to act.
	- People who make a specific plan for when and where they will perform a new habit are more likely to follow through.
	- Format : I will [BEHAVIOR] at [TIME] in [LOCATION].
- Many people think they lack motivation when what they really lack is clarity.
- We often say yes to little requests because we are not clear enough about what we need to be doing instead.

##### HABIT STACKING
- One of the best ways to build a new habit is to identify a current habit you already do each day and then stack your new behavior on top
	- The key is to tie desired behavior into sth u already do each day.
	- Unlike implementation intention, habit stacking already has the time and location built into it.
- Habit Stacking Formula : After I [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT].
	- e.g. After I pour my cup of coffee each morning, I will meditate for one minute.
- How to Habit Stack :
	1) Brainstorm list of current habits(use habit scorecard)
	2) Write a list of things that happen to u w/o fail each day (e.g. Sun rises, Receives texts)
	3) With these 2 lists search for the best place to layer new habits into ur lifestyle
	- The cue needs to be highly specific and immediately actionable


### Chapter 6 : Motivation is Overrated; Environment Often Matters More 
- Environment is the invisible hand that shapes human behavior
- Given that humans are more dependent on vision than on any other sense, it should come as no surprise that visual cues are the greatest catalyst of our behavior
- The most persistent behavior usually have multiple cues. So if you want to make a habit a big part of ur life, make the cue a big part of ur enviroment
- Enviroment design allows u to take back control and become the architect of ur life
- Stop thinking about ur enviroment as filled w/ objects but rather as relationships.
- When u step outside ur normal enviroment, u leave ur behavioral biases behind. 


### Chapter 7 : The Secret to Self-Control
- People who are better at structuring their lives spend less time in tempting situations.
- If you are not careful about cues, u can cause the very behavior u want to stop.
- Self-control is a short-term strategy not a long-term one.
