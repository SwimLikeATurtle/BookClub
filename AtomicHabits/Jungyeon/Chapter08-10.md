## THE SECOND LAW : MAKE IT ATTRACTIVE

### Chapter 8 : How to Make a Habit Irresistable
- **Supernormal Stimuli** : a heightened version of reality that illicits a stronger response than usual
- Today food is abundant, but your brain continues to crave it like it is scarce. 
	- Placing a high value on food is no longer advantageous to our health, but our craving persists because the brains reward centers have not changed for the approximately 50,000 years

##### THE DOPAMINE DRIVEN FEEDBACK LOOP
- Dopamine is released not only when you experience pleasure but also when you anticipate it. Whenever dopamine rises, so does your motivation to act

##### HOW TO USE TEMPTATION BUNDLING TO MAKE YOUR HABITS, MORE ATTRACTIVE
- Temptation bundling is one way to make your habits, more attractive. The strategy is to pair an action you want to do with an action you need to do.


### Chapter 9 : The Role of Family and Friends in Shaping Your Habits
- Whatever habits are normal in your culture are among the most attractive behaviors you’ll find

##### THE SEDUCTIVE PULL OF SOCIAL NORMS
- Often you follow the habits of your culture without thinking without questioning, and sometimes without remembering
	1) Imitating the Close
	- We soak up the qualities and practices of those around us
	- Nothing sustains motivation better than belonging to the tribe. It transforms a personal question into a shared one. 
	- This is why remaining part of a group after achieving a goal is crucial to maintaining your habits.

	2) Imitating the Many
	- Most days we’d rather be wrong with the crowd, then be right by ourselves
	- When changing your habit means fitting in with the tribe change is very attractive

	3) Imitating the Powerful
	- High status people enjoy the approval, respect and praise of others. That means if a behavior get us approval respect and praise we find it attractive.


### Chapter 10 : How to Find and Fix the Causes of Your Bad Habits

##### WHERE CRAVING COME FROM
- Our behavior is heavily dependent on how we interpret the events that happened to us, not necessarily the objective reality of event themselves
- Craving is a sense that something is missing. It is the desire to change your internal state.
- Desire is the difference between where you are now and where you want to be in the future

##### HOW TO REPROGRAM YOUR BRAIN TO ENJOY HARD HABITS
- You can make hard habits, more attractive if you can learn to associate them with a positive experience
- Think : You don’t HAVE to you GET to
- Reframing your habit to highlight their benefits rather than their drawbacks is a fast and lightweight way to reprogram your mind and make a habit seem more attractive
	- Exercise : it’s time to build endurance and get fast
	- Finance : Tamany you saved this month increases your purchasing power next month
	- Meditation: destruction gives me a chance to practice meditation
	- Pregame jitters: I am excited and I am getting adrenaline rush to help me concentrate
- Your habits are modern day solution to ancient desires
- Create a motivation ritual by doing something you enjoy immediately before a difficult habit.
