## ADVANCED TACTICS : HOW TO GO FROM BEING MERELY GOOD TO BEING TRULY GREAT

### Chapter 18: The Truth About Talent
- The secret to maximizing your odds of success is to choose the right field of competition
- Genes cannot be easily changed which makes the strength of genetics is also their weakness.
	- "Genes can predispose but they don't predetermine" Gabor Mate

##### HOW YOUR PERSONALITY INFLUENCES YOUR HABITS
- Your genes are operating beneath the surface of every habit. Indeed, beneath the surface of every behavior. 
- The most proven scientific analysis of personality traits is known as the Big Five
	1) Openness to experience
	2) Conscientiousness
	3) Extroversion
	4) Agreeableness
	5) Neuroticism
- You should build habits that work for your personality.

##### HOW TO FIND A GAME WHERE THE ODDS ARE IN YOUR FAVOR
- How to find the right game:
	- In the beginning of a new activity, there should be a period of exploration. Cast a wide net and try out many possibilities
	- After initial period of exploration, shift your focus to the best solution you've found but keep experimenting occasionally. If you are currently winning, exploit. If you are losing, continue to explore.
	- The best strategy is to deliver the best results 80-90% of the time and keep exploring with the remaining 10-20%
- Questions to ask yourself to narrow in on the habits that will work for you:
	1) What feels like fun to me, but work to others?
	2) What makes me lose track of time?
	3) Where do I get greater returns than the average person?
	4) What comes naturally to me?
- Scott Adams, the cartoonist of Dilbert, says everyone has a few areas in which they are in the top 25%, combine these skills to find your niche	

##### HOW TO GET THE MOST OUT OF YOUR GENES
- Pick habits that align with your personality and skills. Work hard on the things that come easy.


### Chapter 19: The Goldilocks Rule How to Stay Motivated in Life and Work
- The Goldilocks Rule : Humans experience peak motivation when working on tasks that are right on the edge of their current abilities. It has to be just right.
- When you are starting a new habit it's important to keep the behavior as easy as possible so you can stick with it even when conditions aren't perfect.
	- The flow state you want to reach is roughly 4% beyond your current ability.
	- Behavior need to remain novel in order for them to stay attractive and satisfying. Without variety we get bored.

##### HOW TO STAY FOCUSED WHEN YOU GET BORED WORKING ON YOUR GOALS
- At some point being successful comes down to who can handle the boredom of training every day, doing the same thing over and over.
	- Successful people find a way to show up despite the feelings of boredom
- The greatest threat to success is not failure but boredom
	- As our habits become ordinary we start derailing our progress to seek novelty.
	- "Men desire novelty to such an extent that those who are doing well wish for a change as much as those who are doing badly" Machiavelli
- The sweet spot of desire occurs at a 50/50 split between success and failure
- No habits will stay interesting forever, so you have to fall in love with boredom
- Professionals stick to the schedule; amateurs let life get in the way
- The only way to become excellent is to be endlessly fascinated by doing the same thing over and over.

### Chapter 20: The Downside of Creating Good Habits
- You assume you're getting better because you're gaining experience.
- Habits are necessary, but not sufficient for mastery.
	- Habits + Deliberate Practice = Mastery
- After one habit has been mastered, you have to return to the effortful part of the work and begin building the next habit.

##### HOW TO REVIEW YOUR HABITS AND MAKE ADJUSTMENTS
- "Sustaining an effort is the most important thing. The way to be successful is to learn how to do things right then do them the same way every time." Pat Riley
- Without reflection, we can make excuses, create rationalizations, and lie to ourselves. We have no process for determining whether we are performing better or worse compared to yesterday.

- How to reflect and review your habits:
	- Conduct Annual Report to reflect on the previous year
		- Questions to answer when conducting an Annual Report
			1) What went well this year?
			2) What didn't go well this year?
			3) What did I learn?
	- 6M later conduct an Integrity Report
		- Integrity Report helps to revisit my desired identity and indicate when one should upgrade one's habit
		- Questions to answer when conducting an Integrity Report
			1) What are the core values that drive my life and work
			2) How am I living and working with integrity right now?
			3) How can I set a higher standard in the future?

##### HOW TO BREAK THE BELIEFS THAT HOLD YOU BACK
- The more an idea is sacred to us - the more deeply it is tied to our identity - the more strongly we will defend it against criticism
- The more you let a single belief define you, the less capable you are of adapting when life challenges you.
- Redefine yourself in a way that you get to keep important aspects of your identity even if your role changes
	- I am an athlete -> I'm the type of person who is mentally tough and loves a physical challenge
	- I am the CEO -> I'm the type of person who builds and creates things

### Conclusion
- The holy grail of habit change is not a single habit but bunch of them. Its in a bunch of atomic habits stacking up each one a fundamental unit of the overall system
- Success is not a goal to reach or a finish line to cross. It is a system to improve, an endless process to refine.
	- Rotate thru the Four Laws of Behavior Change uintil you find the bottleneck and always look for the next way to get 1% better
