## THE THIRD LAW : MAKE IT EASY

### Chapter 11 : Walk Slowly, but Never Backward
- We are so focused on figuring out the best approach that we never get around to taking action.
	- "The best is the enemy of the good" - Voltaire
- Motion allows us to feel like we're making progress w/o running the risk of failure. Preparation becomes a form of procrastination. You need to stop planning and start practicing.

##### HOW LONG DOES IT ACTUALLY TAKE TO FORM A NEW HABIT?
- Simply putting in your reps is one of the most critical steps you can take to encoding a new habit
- The important truth about behavior change : Habits form based on frequency, not time.


### Chapter 12 : The Law of Least Effort
- Conventional wisdom holds that motivation is key to change. But real motivation of humans is to be lazy and to do what is convenient
- The less friction you face, the easier it is for your stronger self to emerge.

##### HOW TO ACHIEVE MORE WITH LESS EFFORT
- Trying to pump up motivation to stick with a hard habit is like trying to force water through a bent hose You can do it, but it requires a lot of effort and increases the tension in your life. 
	- Making habits, simple, and easy is like removing the bent in the hose. 
- To remove friction, one must design its environment
	- It is best to choose a place that is along the path of your daily routine. 
- Addition by subtraction: looking for every point of friction in the process and eliminating it. (e.g. Japanese lean production)
- Business is a never-ending quest to deliver the same result in an easier fashion

##### PRIME THE ENVIRONMENT FOR FUTURE USE
- Redesign your life so the actions that matter most are also the actions that are easiest to do


### Chapter 13 : How to Stop Procrastinating by Using the Two Minute Rule
- Researchers estimate that 40-50% of our actions on any given day are done out of habit
- Habits are automatic choices that influence the conscious decision decisions that follow
- Decisive moments set the options available to your future self
- We are limited by where our habits lead us

##### THE TWO MINUTE RULE
- Two minute rule: when you start a new habit, it should take less than two minutes to do
	- e.g. Read before bed each night -> Read one page
- Once you’ve started doing the right thing, it is much easier to continue doing it 
	- A new habit should not feel like a challenge. The actions that follow can be challenging, but the first two minutes should be easy.
- The point is to master the habit of showing up. A habit must be established before it can be improved.
- If the two minute rule feels forced, try doing it for two minutes and then stop. Stop before it seems like work or like a hassle.  


### Chapter 14 : HOW TO MAKE GOOD HABITS, INEVITABLE, AND BAD HABITS IMPOSSIBLE
- Sometimes success is less about making good habits, easy and more about making bad habits hard. 
- One can make bad habits, more difficult by creating a commitment device
- Commitment Device: a choice you make in the present that controls your action in the future. 

##### HOW TO AUTOMATE A HABIT AND NEVER THINK ABOUT IT AGAIN
- By utilizing commitment devices, strategic onetime decisions, and technology, you can create an environment of inevitability - a space where good habits are not just an outcome you hope for but an outcome that is virtually guaranteed.
- Civilization advances by extending the number of operations we can perform without thinking about them - Alfred North Whitehead
